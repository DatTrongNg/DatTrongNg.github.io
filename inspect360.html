How it works

Behind the viewer

A vehicle is filmed on a turntable and the video is decomposed into a fixed set of evenly-spaced frames. Dragging maps horizontal cursor travel to a frame index, so the car appears to rotate in real time. A full-width drag sweeps roughly half a turn.

On release, the viewer samples the last ~120 ms of pointer movement to estimate spin velocity, then carries it into an ease-out glide whose initial speed matches your hand — so the hand-off is seamless and the spin decelerates to a clean stop rather than halting abruptly.
